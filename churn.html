  • Real-time Monitoring: Continuously monitors system and model health to ensure accurate predictions.

Solution

Solution Architecture

How we technically deliver the objectives.

  • Churn Score: Provides a real-time risk score for each customer to trigger retention actions.
  • Customer Metrics: Tracks average spend, tenure, and engagement patterns for insights.
  • Behavioral Analytics: Monitors usage patterns, interaction frequency, and support tickets.
  • Integration: The product integrates seamlessly with the company CRM system to push risk scores, alerts, and actionable insights directly to agent dashboards.
  • Alternative Approach: Rule-based predictions can serve as a fallback for initial MVP testing or if the ML model is unavailable.
5

Constraints & Limitations

Technical and operational boundaries we must work within.

  • Constraints:Real-time churn prediction, low latency (<100ms)
  • Timeline: 3-week MVP delivery deadline
  • Security: PII anonymization, RBAC, audit logging required
  • Out-of-scope: Direct customer communication (SMS, Email), which will be handled by the CRM system.
Design decisions must balance innovation with practical constraints of team size and timeline.

Data Sources

Describe the training and production sources of data.

  • Training: CRM, historical churn labels, billing system, service logs.
  • Production: Usage events, session logs, billing events.
  • External: Third-party engagement logs where available.
7

Labeling Process

Describe the labeling process and decisions.

  • Labels:Churned customers are marked as 1 for churn and 0 otherwise in the historical data.
  • Reality: No explicit churn flags; churn must be inferred from inactivity.
  • Decisions: Define a reasonable inactivity threshold to label customers as churned for model training, based on company context.
Focus on dataset labeling and versionsing for scalability and reproducibility.

Metrics

Tie core objectives with quantitative metrics that our model can optimize towards.

  • Business: Measures customer retention rate and computes churn rate to assess overall business impact.
  • ML: Evaluates model performance using ROC-AUC, PR-AUC, Precision, Recall, and F1-score to ensure accurate churn detection.
  • System: Monitors latency, throughput, and error rate to validate real-time prediction performance.
Focus on setting baselines for MVP.

Evaluation

When and how we'll evaluate our model.

  • Offline: Use stratified cross-validation to ensure robustness and handle class imbalance during model training.
  • Online: A/B tests (out-of-scope).
  • Continuous: Track key model metrics over time, comparing against baseline and historical performance.
  • Monitoring: Integrate technical alerts (e.g., data drift, label drift, performance drop) with business monitoring to identify real-world impacts of model changes.
Combine alerts with business monitoring to detect real impact from model changes.

Modeling Approach

Core models to use.

  • Model families: Logistic Regression, Random Forest, XGBoost/LightGBM, Neural Network.
  • Approach: Begin with interpretable models (e.g., Logistic Regression, Random Forest) to ensure explainability and faster iteration.
  • Scalability: Gradually introduce more complex models (e.g., XGBoost, Neural Network) for performance improvement after baseline evaluation.
Prioritize simpler models with strong explainability for pilot deployment.
